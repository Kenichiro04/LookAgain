Builder readiness & roadmap

Look Again is designed as a focused Android XR prototype, not a broad museum-platform launch. The goal is to test whether short, source-grounded pointing cues can help people know where to look before they know what to ask.

Why this can be built

Rapid prototype

Browser prototype now, native Android XR prototype next.

Measurable interaction

Test where-to-look clarity, phone comparison, and do-not-remove-glasses rate.

Source-grounded content

Use public-domain artworks and vetted source notes; AI routes cues, not invented interpretations.

Small enough to ship

The MVP avoids museum-wide navigation, heavy 3D reconstruction, and partnership dependency.

6-month roadmap

  1. Month 1

    Native Android XR project setup.

    Port browser cue model into Android / Jetpack XR prototype.

  2. Month 2

    Artwork recognition / selection flow.

    Implement 3 public-domain artwork packs.

  3. Month 3

    Spatial cue rendering.

    Pointer, pulse, connector, peripheral panel, disappear-by-default behavior.

  4. Month 4

    Project Aura validation.

    Low-light visibility, color fidelity, comfort, distraction, anchoring.

  5. Month 5

    User study.

    Compare no guide / audio-like context / phone guide / Look Again cue.

  6. Month 6

    Closed test package.

    Demo video, Google Play closed testing readiness, public documentation.

Beyond museums

Museums are the first proof environment because the problem is visible there: people can be looking at the real thing and still not know where to look.

The larger vision is a pre-search pointing layer for the physical world. Before a person knows what to ask, Android XR can point to one meaningful detail, ask one grounded question, and disappear.
