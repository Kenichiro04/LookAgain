Why this needs XR

This is not about putting more text in glasses. The value is that a perspective can appear on the real artwork while you keep looking at it.

01

A brief visual perspective

A short overlay appears on one visible detail, then fades.

02

Attached to the artwork

The guidance belongs to a visible detail, not to a floating label.

03

Place and explanation stay connected

The marked detail connects to a short explanation at the edge of view.

04

Movement only when useful

Side viewing is used only for material or surface questions.

05

It fades from view

The display stays brief, then returns attention to the artwork.

Museum-first, not museum-only

Museums are the first test because the problem is easy to see there. Later, the same pattern could apply in other settings where people are looking, but do not yet know what to ask.

Scenographer view Sightline / scenery layers Scenographer reading The set creates depth by stacking flat scenery in the viewer’s sightline.

Opera stage set

Sightlines and scenery layers explain how the stage creates space before the viewer knows the craft terms.

Photographer view Foreground / ridge / haze Photographer reading Foreground rocks anchor scale; repeating ridges move the eye into depth.

Landscape photography

Composition guides and depth layers show how a photograph leads attention through a real scene.

Heritage conservator view Original stone / repair seam Conservator reading Different stone courses separate original fabric from later repair.

Cultural heritage site

Material seams and missing fragments make the history of the site readable on the site itself.

Future contexts are examples of the interaction pattern, not current product scope.
